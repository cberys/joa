About Dr. James Swift

Dr. James Swift is a research oceanographer at the Scripps Institution of Oceanography. His research interests include Arctic physical oceanography, ocean measurement and interpretation, and World Ocean water masses and circulation. He helps coordinate academic field activities for the US Global Ocean Carbon and Repeat Hydrography Program and is Director of the CLIVAR and Carbon Hydrographic Data Office. He created this suite of ocean data exploration tools for teaching and research.

About Java OceanAtlas

Java OceanAtlas was developed by oceanographic applications specialist John "Oz" Osborne (OceanAtlas Software, Vashon Island, Washington), working with Dr. James Swift. Read about the Background of Java OceanAtlas.

About the Data

The data on this site were assembled by Dr. James Swift from sources including the CLIVAR and Carbon Hydrographic Data Office, the Reid/Mantyla data set, Syd Levitus' World Ocean Atlas data sets, as well as his own research files. Matthew Shen and Roxanne Lee created the WOA09 and WOA05 subset files (respectively), including the WOA sections. The data archive is maintained by Matthew Shen.
